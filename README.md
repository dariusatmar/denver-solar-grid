# The Case for Grid-Scale Solar in Denver

A data-driven argument for Denver to build utility-scale solar farms on Colorado's Front Range.

Inspired by Casey Handmer's [Energy Predictions 2025](https://caseyhandmer.wordpress.com/2025/12/08/energy-predictions-2025/) and grounded in real data from NREL, EIA, Lazard, and the Colorado energy market.

## Key Arguments

- **Solar LCOE ($24–31/MWh)** is now cheaper than *operating* existing coal and gas plants
- **Denver's DNI (6.12 kWh/m²/day)** beats Miami, LA, and 90% of the US
- **Colorado's grid is 63% fossil** with 958 lbs CO₂/MWh — 16th worst in the nation
- **Eastern Plains land** is cheap, flat, and close to Front Range load centers
- **Battery storage** makes solar fully dispatchable and kills gas peakers
- **Datacenters** are building captive solar plants — Denver can attract them
- **Federal ITC (30%)** still available for utility-scale through 2032
- **Synthetic fuels** from solar power could make Colorado a net energy exporter
